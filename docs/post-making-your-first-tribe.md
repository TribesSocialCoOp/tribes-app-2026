# Making Your First Tribe

**Tribe:** Welcome to Tribes

---

You signed up. You're looking around. Now what?

Make a tribe. Seriously. It takes about two minutes and it's the fastest way to understand what Tribes is actually for.

### Public or Private?

Before you start, decide what you're building:

**Public tribes** are open forums. Anyone can find them, read them, and join. Think of these like a town square, a subreddit, or a community board. If you want to start a conversation that anyone can participate in, go public. Public tribes show up in Discover and their posts can be shared and linked anywhere.

**Private tribes** are encrypted and invite-only. Only members can see the content. The tribe founder (you) holds the encryption key and controls who gets access. Think of these like a group chat that actually works. Your family, your team, your friend group, your book club, your project collaborators. If the conversation isn't for everyone, go private.

You can always change visibility later in tribe settings.

### Creating Your Tribe: Step by Step

Head to [tribes.app/tribes](https://tribes.app/tribes) and hit **Create New Tribe** (or go directly to [tribes.app/tribes/create](https://tribes.app/tribes/create)).

You'll see a simple form:

1. **Tribe Name**: Pick something clear. "Weekend Hikers PDX" is better than "Hiking." You can get creative, but make sure people understand what the tribe is about at a glance.

2. **Homepage URL** (optional): If your tribe represents an existing group, project, or organization, link to your website here.

3. **Moods** (pick 1 to 3): Moods are how Tribes organizes content across the platform. Pick the moods that best describe your tribe's vibe. If your tribe is about tech, pick Tech. If it's about wellness, pick Wellness. These help people discover your tribe and help your posts surface in the right mood streams.

4. **Description**: Write a short pitch for your tribe. What's it about? Who's it for? What should members expect? This shows up on your tribe's page and in search results. Keep it honest.

5. **Cover Image** (optional): Upload a banner image. It shows at the top of your tribe page. Makes a big difference for first impressions.

6. **Visibility Toggle**: Public (discoverable by anyone) or Private (invite-only, encrypted). This is the big decision. Pick what fits.

Hit **Create Tribe** and you're live.

### Your First Post

Don't overthink it. Your first post should be a welcome message. Tell people what the tribe is about, what kind of content you're looking for, and set the tone. Are you casual? Professional? Weird? Let people know what they're getting into.

Here's a template if you're stuck:

> "Welcome to [tribe name]. This is a space for [topic/group]. Post about [what you want to see]. Keep it [your vibe]. Looking forward to building this with you."

That's it. Post it and move on.

### Inviting People

Right now, the best way to get people into your tribe is to share the link. Every tribe has a shareable URL. Copy it from your browser bar or use the share button on any post.

For private tribes, people will need to request to join and you'll approve them from the manage members page. You'll get a notification when someone requests access.

For public tribes, anyone with the link can join immediately.

### Moderation

As the tribe founder, you have moderation tools:

- **Pin posts** to the top of the feed so important content stays visible
- **Remove posts** that violate your tribe's standards
- **Review reported content** from the mod queue
- **Manage members** from the tribe settings

You set the culture. Moderation isn't about control, it's about maintaining the space you want to be in. A well-moderated tribe with 20 engaged members is worth more than an unmoderated tribe with 2,000 lurkers.

### Ideas to Get You Started

Not sure what tribe to create? Here are some ideas:

- **Your friend group**: Replace that group chat that's been going for 5 years with something that actually has threads, images, and isn't owned by Meta
- **A local community**: Your neighborhood, your city's music scene, your running club
- **A project team**: Private tribe for your startup, your band, your volunteer org
- **A topic you care about**: Woodworking, AI ethics, indie games, urban planning, cooking experiments
- **Your family**: A private, encrypted space for family updates that isn't Facebook
